Theoretical & Conceptual Framework

Establishing the foundational theories and introducing the comprehensive Five-Pillar Framework designed for this study.

Financial expenditure is meaningless without an effective communication architecture.

The true impact of CSR is determined by HOW it is communicated, not just by the money spent.

The Output Fallacy

"We built 10 schools."

Measures activity and expenditure, but not the actual change in human lives.

The Outcome Approach

"Literacy increased by 15%."

Measures actual societal betterment and capacity building.

"Development requires the removal of major sources of unfreedom: poverty as well as tyranny, poor economic opportunities as well as systematic social deprivation."

Why These Four Theories?

Stakeholder Theory: Defines tribal communities as primary stakeholders, not secondary
Two-Way Symmetrical: Tests whether dialogue outperforms one-way messaging
Agenda-Setting: Tests whether corporate priorities match community needs
Diffusion of Innovations: Maps the adoption curve in low-literacy tribal settings

How the Framework Applies to This Study

  • The Five-Pillar Framework maps the complete lifecycle of every CSR initiative in the sample
  • Pillar 5 (Communication Strategy) is the independent variable measured by the CES
  • Beneficiary perception is the dependent variable captured through 400 structured interviews
  • State (CG vs JH) is the primary intervening variable for comparative analysis
