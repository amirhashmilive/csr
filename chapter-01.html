Rationale for State Selection

Similarities

  • Both formed in the year 2000
  • High concentration of tribal populations
  • Mineral-rich geographies
  • Dominated by heavy extractive industries

Differences

  • Governance histories and stability
  • Development indicator trajectories
  • CSR implementation approaches
  • Corporate-community relationship dynamics

Why a Comparative Analysis?

While individual state studies exist, a comparative lens isolates the communication variable. Since both states share similar industrial and demographic profiles, differing outcomes in CSR satisfaction can be more confidently attributed to how companies communicate with beneficiaries rather than what they spend.

Significance of the Study

Academic

Fills the research gap by applying communication theories to rural/tribal contexts in India.

Policy

Provides empirical evidence for the Ministry of Corporate Affairs to refine CSR reporting guidelines.

Practical

Offers an actionable framework for corporates and NGOs to improve beneficiary engagement.

Key Terms Defined

CSR

Mandatory corporate allocation of profits for societal welfare under Section 135.

CSR Communication

The strategic flow of information between a company and its stakeholders regarding its social initiatives.

Beneficiary

The ultimate end-user or community member who receives the impact of a CSR project.

Betterment of Society

Measurable improvements in education, healthcare, environment, and livelihood.

CES

Communication Effectiveness Score, a composite metric developed for this study.

Five-Pillar Framework

The conceptual model analyzing the lifecycle of CSR from entity to communication.
