Chapter 05

Research Methodology

A Pragmatist, Abductive, Explanatory Sequential Mixed-Method Communication Research Study employing a Retrospective Longitudinal Comparative Design.

Mixed-Methods Research Design

Quantitative

Structured Surveys
N = 400

Pragmatism

Triangulation

Qualitative

In-depth Interviews
N = 40

Combining statistical rigor to identify what is happening, with thematic depth to understand why it is happening.

Research Philosophy

Pragmatism

Not purely Positivist

Because human perception of "betterment of society" cannot be perfectly quantified as an absolute objective truth.

Not purely Interpretivist

Because CSR implementation needs measurable, generalizable data to form actionable policy recommendations.

Multi-stage Stratified Sampling

1

Geographical Selection

Purposive selection of 5 districts in each state based on highest concentration of industrial and CSR activity.

2

Sectoral Stratification

Stratifying projects into Education, Health, Environment, and Livelihood to ensure diverse representation.

3

Random Beneficiary Selection

Simple random sampling of end-users within the selected project areas.

Data Collection

Primary Sources

  • Structured Surveys: Bilingual (Hindi/English) Likert-scale questionnaires for 400 beneficiaries.
  • In-depth Interviews: Semi-structured discussions with 40 stakeholders (CSR Heads, NGO Reps, Govt Officials).
  • Field Observations: Conducted across 10 districts over 6 months.

Secondary Sources

  • National CSR Portal: Expenditure data from Ministry of Corporate Affairs.
  • Corporate Reports: Annual Reports, Business Responsibility Reports (BRSR).
  • Academic Databases: Scopus, Web of Science, Google Scholar.

Ethical Considerations

Informed Consent

Written or recorded verbal consent obtained before all surveys and interviews.

Anonymity

Personal identifiers removed. Beneficiaries assured that negative feedback would not impact aid.

Data Security

Raw data securely stored offline. Aggregate data reported to prevent triangulation of individuals.

Limitations of the Study

Challenge in isolating individual corporate impact in regions where multiple companies operate simultaneously.

Logistical dependency on company/NGO coordination to access secure remote tribal areas.

Nuance loss during translation from local tribal dialects (Gondi/Santhali) to Hindi/English.

Cross-sectional data collection prevents longitudinal measurement of impact evolution.

Delimitations

Geographical

Confined strictly to Chhattisgarh and Jharkhand.

Sectoral

Limited to Education, Health, Environment, and Livelihood.

Stakeholder Scope

Focuses on direct beneficiaries; excludes general public perception.

Variable Focus

Focuses exclusively on the communication aspect, not financial auditing.

Theoretical Scope

Bound by the newly proposed Five-Pillar Framework.

Regulatory Framework

Evaluated strictly under the parameters of Companies Act, 2013.
