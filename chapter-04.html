Research Objectives & Hypotheses

Defining the research aims and establishing the testable hypotheses through the Five-Pillar Framework.

Addressing the 5 Research Gaps

No comparative study of CG vs JH

Corporate-centric, not beneficiary-centric

Theories untested in tribal contexts

No mixed-methods studies in this region

Banking sector CSR not analyzed

Research Objectives

O1: Primary Objective

To compare the effectiveness of CSR communication strategies between companies operating in Chhattisgarh and Jharkhand.

O2: Education

To assess the role of CSR communication in the educational development of beneficiaries.

O3: Healthcare

To evaluate the influence of CSR communication on healthcare access and awareness.

O4: Environment

To examine the contribution of CSR communication in promoting environmental conservation.

O5: Livelihood

To analyse the role of CSR communication in enhancing livelihood opportunities.

Research Hypotheses

H₁

There is a significant difference in the Communication Effectiveness Score (CES) between Chhattisgarh and Jharkhand.

Tested via Independent t-test
H₂

Effective CSR communication significantly increases the likelihood of perceived educational improvement.

Tested via Logistic Regression
H₃

There is a significant interaction effect between state geography and communication channels on healthcare perception.

Tested via Two-Way ANOVA
H₄

There is a significant association between the type of communication channel used and environmental impact recognition.

Tested via Chi-Square
H₅

Communication Effectiveness is a stronger predictor of perceived livelihood enhancement than geographical state.

Tested via Multiple Linear Regression

Expected Outcomes

Theoretical Contributions

  • Validation of the Five-Pillar Framework.
  • Empirical proof that communication is an independent driver of CSR success.
  • Adaptation of PR models to tribal/rural demographics.

Policy Implications

  • Recommendations for the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA).
  • Guidelines for linguistic inclusion in CSR reporting.
  • Actionable framework for NGOs and Corporate Foundations.
